Why This Content Works
Why people follow
They're embarrassed to ask anyone in their real life. Meg becomes the person they can't have IRL. Once someone feels "she gets me," the follow is automatic. They're not following for information. They're following for permission.
Why people share
Two triggers: private relatability ("I sent this to my partner and said nothing") and collective vindication ("everyone needs to see this"). Content that names something shameful and immediately normalizes it hits both. The share is the person's way of opening a conversation they couldn't start themselves.
What makes someone stay vs scroll
The first three seconds need to either call them out specifically or say something slightly unexpected. People scroll when content feels generic. They stay when they feel personally addressed. Meg's natural tone does this without trying — the goal is just to get it on camera consistently.

People start recognizing it within two videos. They come back wondering "will she say my thing?" and share it when she does.
